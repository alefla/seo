About Me and Web Development

Become a great web designer/web developer is my new goal. It is a new goal that surprised myself on how I like it so much now. I am an artist, and I thought I don't have any interest on website coding. They seem difficult and boring. Last semester I took Digital Skill Visual Media Art course and it cover very short html/css with a simple web design project. That very short time change my perspective about coding, specially html/css. The result is a piece of art too! They are beautiful and I still can be creative!

After last semester done, my winter break filled with study of html and css whenever I had break within family and work. Everyday I learned something new from the internet. I am glad that there are so many resources and free online classes. Although sometimes can be confusing when reading blogs as they are can be an individual opinion. I found the best resource is W3Schools. It has become my favorite because they have my answer of all my questions regarding html/css and the best part is the actual simple example to run. We can edit and run it in their small editor box. That's great to play around so we can understand better.

The best way to learn is by taking a formal education path and the internets are the additional as it is never enough to learn from one place. CCSF courses are free for San Francisco resident and it also has an online courses. I would recommend to check CCSF Counselor before signing up the class. Please make an appointment to see a counselor to get all the information that you need and some advice.

Good Links

  • MDN mozilla
    MDN mozilla provides information for complete beginners to web development with all that they need to start coding websites. It tells you where to start, where to go for question and they want you to get comfortable with the coding. You get what you need pretty much right there.
  • freeCodeCamp
    They have good learning system. You are learning by doing exercise and the system will warn you if you made mistakes. The great thing is you will get a certificate if pass all of the challenges.
  • Stack Overflow
    Stack Overflow is an open community for anyone that codes. They help you get answers to your toughest coding questions, and people share their knowledge. Most of their questions are real and the answer came from people that actually try to help them. So it's more practical when we hit a problem rather than searching the answer in articles/blogs.
